The Old City

The Old City of Jerusalem is a historic walled area within modern Jerusalem, containing sites of immense religious and cultural significance to Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Divided into four quarters—Jewish, Christian, Muslim, and Armenian—the Old City spans just one square kilometer but holds thousands of years of history. Key landmarks include the Western Wall, the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, and the Dome of the Rock. The ancient stone walls and narrow alleyways create a unique atmosphere where different cultures and religions coexist. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Old City attracts millions of pilgrims and tourists annually who come to experience its sacred sites, bustling markets, and rich archaeological heritage.
