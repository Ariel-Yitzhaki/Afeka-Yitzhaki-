Times Square

Times Square is a major commercial intersection and entertainment hub located in Midtown Manhattan, New York City. Known as "The Crossroads of the World," it is famous for its massive digital billboards, Broadway theaters, and bustling atmosphere that attracts over 50 million visitors annually. The area became iconic in the early 20th century as the heart of New York's theater district and has evolved into one of the world's most recognized landmarks. Times Square is particularly famous for its New Year's Eve ball drop celebration, watched by millions worldwide. The constant flow of pedestrians, bright neon lights, and vibrant energy make it a symbol of New York City's dynamic culture and a must-visit destination for tourists from around the globe.
